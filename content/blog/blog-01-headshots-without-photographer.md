## Option 2: DIY Smartphone Photography

Yes, you can take professional-looking headshots with your phone. But let me be real: this requires effort and probably several attempts.

### Equipment You Need

**Essential:**
- Smartphone with portrait mode (iPhone 8+ or equivalent)
- Tripod or stable surface
- Timer app or Bluetooth remote

**Recommended:**
- Ring light ($20-40) or natural window light
- Plain background (white wall, poster board)
- Someone to help with positioning

### The DIY Process

**Step 1: Set Up Lighting**

The #1 factor in professional-looking photos is lighting. You have two good options:

*Natural light:* Position yourself facing a large window. The light should hit your face evenly—no harsh shadows across your nose or under your eyes. Overcast days are actually ideal.

*Ring light:* Place it directly in front of your face, slightly above eye level. This creates that even, flattering light you see in professional headshots.

**Step 2: Background**

Keep it simple. A plain white or light gray wall works for most purposes. Make sure there's no clutter, outlets, or picture frames visible.

If your walls are busy, hang a solid-colored bedsheet or buy a $15 photography backdrop from Amazon.

**Step 3: Camera Settings**

- Use portrait mode for natural background blur
- Turn on HDR
- Set timer for 3-10 seconds
- Enable burst mode to capture multiple shots

**Step 4: Positioning**

- Camera at eye level or slightly above
- Turn your body 15-30 degrees (not straight-on)
- Chin slightly forward and down (eliminates double chin)
- Relax your shoulders
- Think of something genuinely funny right before the shot (natural smile)

**Step 5: Take 50+ Photos**

Seriously. Professional photographers take hundreds of shots to get a few keepers. Don't expect to nail it in 5 tries.

### DIY Limitations

Let me be honest about what's hard to replicate:

- **Professional lighting setups** - Studio photographers use multiple lights
- **Lens quality** - Phone cameras still can't match professional glass
- **Expert direction** - Photographers know how to position you
- **Advanced retouching** - Skin smoothing, color grading

## What Actually Matters in a Professional Headshot

Before you choose a method, understand what makes a headshot "professional":

### 1. Lighting

Even, flattering light with no harsh shadows. This is 70% of what makes a photo look professional.

### 2. Focus

Sharp focus on your eyes, with gentle background blur (bokeh). This draws attention to your face.

### 3. Expression

Natural, approachable smile. Not too serious, not too goofy. Think "friendly competence."

### 4. Framing

Head and shoulders, with some space above your head. Not too tight, not too loose.

### 5. Background

Simple, non-distracting. Solid colors or gentle blur work best.

### 6. Attire

Clean, professional clothing appropriate for your industry. Solid colors photograph better than patterns.

### 7. Retouching

Subtle enhancements: skin smoothing, color correction, blemish removal. Nothing that makes you unrecognizable.
